Design Language

Calma

Philosophy

Every decision should pass one test: does this feel considered and human, or does it feel like software? Prefer the former. White space is not empty — it is calm. Typography is the primary design material. Color is used sparingly and always semantically.

Palette

Built on the stone scale — warm off-whites and near-blacks rather than cold greys or pure blacks. Feels like paper and ink, not a screen. A single warm accent carries all emotional and action weight. Red is reserved for errors only.

Typography

A single typeface throughout. Nothing competes. Headings use light weight and wide tracking — nothing feels heavy or urgent.

The section label

The single most consistent typographic element. Every section of every page uses it without exception. Uppercase, widest tracking, muted color. It creates a visual rhythm that unifies the whole system.

Spacing & Layout

Sections breathe. Use generous vertical space — cramped layouts feel anxious. Major thematic breaks use a hairline border divider with equal padding on either side.

Mobile-first

Max content width: 448px. Horizontal padding: 20px. Lines stay short and readable on any phone without feeling cramped on larger screens.

Interaction

Every interactive element transitions its colors on hover and active. Motion is subtle — color shifts only, no movement. Hover always shifts darker in light mode, lighter in dark mode.

Symbols & states

When a symbol needs to communicate two states — resting and active — use an outlined form for the default and a filled amber form for the selected state. The transition between them should feel considered, not mechanical. Any simple shape can carry this pattern: the example below uses a five-petal form. Tap to toggle.

Touch targets

Minimum 44×44px on all tappable elements. Disabled elements are dimmed, never hidden — absence without explanation is confusing.

Shape

Border radius follows a simple hierarchy based on element scale. Consistency matters more than the specific values — the system should feel rounded and approachable, never boxy, never so round it feels playful.

Writing & Microcopy

Words are design material. They should feel as considered as any visual element. No exclamation marks. No all-caps except the section label pattern. Labels are plain and human.
